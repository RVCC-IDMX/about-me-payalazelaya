Biography!

Hello there, my name is Paola and I'm an aspiring web developer at RVCC. My interest for web development started with a web design class that I took in my freshman year of high school. This web design class introduced to me the basic concepts of HTML and CSS, along with some of the elements of multimedia, such as text, image, and animation. My teacher made the lessons that we did each week interesting to learn about and we were able to incorporate the topics that we learned into our assignments that we were assigned. I still remember how for our midterm and final exam, we had to do a practicum of mimicking a sample web page and being able to create our own web page from a topic of our choice. I always had fun doing the assignments that my teacher assigned to us, since they were always easy and straighforward to do, along with how they let us express our creativity too. Our boundary of sticking to the minimum was never there, so my teacher let us to be free with what we would do, as long as it was school appropriate of course.

Before I made a choice of pursuing a major in web development, my initial thought was to major in computer science, but even though I knew about the dilemma with how there isn't enough women in the computer science field, I also passed that assumption along to how there isn't enough women in web development either. The strive to change the current statistics of another demanding tech field is something that I wish to establish for myself and for other women as well.
